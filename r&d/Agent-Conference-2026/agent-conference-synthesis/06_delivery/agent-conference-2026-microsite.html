Method signal

Human-agent capture was part of the finding.

The conference was captured as a human-agent research pair. The human sensed the room. The agent carried normalization, extraction, comparison, caveat preservation, and synthesis.

Human role

Choose rooms, sense credibility, notice energy, record audio, photograph slides, and decide what felt actionable in motion.

Agent role

Reconcile schedules, index sources, extract claims, compare talks, preserve caveats, and turn the firehose into inspectable intelligence.

Operating controls

Validation is the new throughput constraint.

Across coding-agent, validation, infrastructure, data, and MCP material, the pattern is the same: AI accelerates candidate work first. The organization has to scale review, gates, sweeps, replay, evals, and incident response around that new volume.

Gates

Known failure modes before promotion: criticality policy, security checks, test selection, coverage ratchets, migration guards, CODEOWNERS, and destructive-action review.

Sweeps

Recurring inspections for drift, repeated review patterns, cross-cutting issues, trace anomalies, prompt-injection evidence, and eval variance.

Replay

Run history, source references, tool calls, actor identity, policy decisions, and output lineage need to be inspectable after the fact.

Human taste

Humans remain responsible for standards, authority, edge-case judgment, and whether the work should exist at all.

Low-regret moves

Start bounded. Build reusable control.

1. Pick one bounded toil pilot

Named owner, measurable success criteria, small blast radius, proposal-first or read-only starting mode.

2. Define a capability registry standard

Owner, contract, side-effect class, permissions, actor policy, audit fields, rollback path, and criticality level.

3. Establish identity and gateway policy

Subject/actor separation, delegated authority, scoped permissions, prompt-injection response, and destructive-action handling.

4. Treat context provenance as platform infrastructure

Source ID, version, permission, freshness, index version, and output references.

5. Turn evals into guardrails

Variance checks, repeatability checks, replay, gates, sweeps, and incident-response loops.

6. Capture domain rules

Reusable skills, domain-rule libraries, encoded decision procedures, and capability policies.
